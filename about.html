About EMI Childcare Center

Emilia N. Obiekea
Center Director & Owner

Emilia N. Obiekea opened the center in January of 2019. She holds degrees in Early Childhood Education and Sociology. During this exciting stage in your child’s early education, our mission is to support, encourage, nurture and develop your child.

We are a family-oriented center aiming to encourage and enhance our students with a high-quality education. Partnering with parents during your child’s educational journey and utilizing our educational approach and researched curriculum are winning combinations to achieve success. Stimulating curiosity, exploration and encouraging questions are an integral part of learning.

Enrolling Now!

Our Goals

Our goal is to bridge the transition from home to a school learning environment. We intend to make the transition from Emi Childcare Center into elementary school as seamless as possible for each of our students upon graduation.

We begin American Sign Language (ASL) from infancy in our Explorers class. Spanish Emersion (SE) is introduced to the toddlers in our Pathfinders class. When children reach preschool, the Globetrotters class, they are fully immersed in ASL and SE along with an introduction to pre-kindergarten course work.

Our teaching style is fun and academic!
We also focus on teaching the students to grow socially and emotionally in a positive atmosphere that is fully conducive to learning and personal growth. We truly make learning fun!

Our Teachers

Must complete a 30-day onboarding process and in-class training. New hires are mentored throughout the process of their first 90 days. This is training done in the ECC way.

All of our educators must pass all state and federal background checks.

CPR and First Aid Certified

Complete Professional Development and continued education is a requirement as we strive to maximize each child’s early learning experience.

“Our teachers are fun, engaging and dedicated professionals that exceed the daycare requirements for employment. We highlight their individual talents to aid in our program growing as a whole.” -Emilia N. Obiekea

Why select Emi Childcare Center?

Emi Childcare Center is licensed by the State of Alabama’s Department of Human Resources.

Our educators receive great on-site training and obtain professional development hours quarterly and annually.

Our curriculum is developmentally appropriate and the approach is well rounded for each class to ensure kindergarten readiness.

Safety is a top priority. Knowing this, We have gone to lengths to ensure entry to the building is restricted to staff and parents only. Door codes are not given out and all entries must be buzzed in by office personnel. The building has been constructed with everyone’s safety in mind.

Open door policy for all parents/guardians.

Our Educational Approach

Our curriculum is designed to be informative and engaging for all ages and stages of our students.

We bridge the transition from home to school with our pleasantly calm environment. A great amount of thought has been put into making each space enjoyable for an optimal learning experience.

High quality furnishings indoor and outdoor to inspire learning and cultivate each child’s development. Charming learning centers in each classroom.

Indoor and Outdoor classes are provided. Garden to teach children about the origin of our food. They enjoy the farm to table aspect of growing and learning about vegetables.

Introduction to foreign language through Spanish emersion. American Sign Language is taught from infancy and expanded upon throughout each class.

Supporting Families

Our families are a part of our communities from across Madison County, AL. We partner with parents to create an environment ensuring their child’s growth and learning experiences are as expected.
Families are supported as their children transition into different classes and achieve milestones. Information is provided to families about locating support for tuition assistance and other community resources.

Enrichments & Activities

We grow beyond the regular core curriculum. The following are included in tuition:

S.T.E.A.M. learning: Science, Technology, Engineering, Art & Math Exploring these concepts on a daily basis through experiments, art projects, early math concepts

Arts & Crafts: Creativity is imperative for well-rounded children. It is an important part of learning to encourage imagination through expression and use of different mediums and materials.

Handwriting: Pre-writing skills are developed beginning in our Pathfinders program. It is expanded to learning to write ABCs, numbers, words and simple sentences from Globetrotters and up.

Second Language: Spanish and American Sign Language is taught through fun lessons, games, songs and made relatable through everyday life activities for our students.

Farm to Table: Class garden is used to teach children about the origin of food by digging, planting, watering and harvesting our plants as they mature.
